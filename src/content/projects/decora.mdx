## What it is

Decora is a Flask app with two pages: a form for a room image URL and a furniture type, and a results page with four product tiles. Gemini looks at the room photo and extracts its style, colours, mood, and search keywords, `apiloader.py` searches the IKEA (AU) catalogue with those keywords, and Gemini scores up to ten candidate products against the room. The best four come back as the recommendation.

## Why I built it

I wanted to wire a vision model into a real decision, not just a chat box. Furniture matching fit: there's a public catalogue to search, and "does this look right in this room" is a judgment call a vision model can score.

## Decisions that mattered

### One text description instead of two images per comparison

I tried sending Gemini the room image and each product image together for a direct visual comparison (commit `a6330a5`), then reverted it the same day (`65a7eb2`). The kept design has `analyse_room()` run once and return a short text description (style, colours, mood), and `score_product()` rates each candidate against that description instead of the room photo, one Gemini call up front instead of re-sending the room image on every scoring call.

### Scoring with a qualify-and-fallback cutoff

`sender.py` scores up to 10 candidates, keeps anything scoring 6 or higher, and sorts by score. If nothing clears 6, it falls back to the top 4 by score instead of returning nothing (fixed in `42b857a`, which also fixed a pandas NaN check where a missing image URL was truthy and never triggered the fallback it was meant to).

## What I would do differently

`score_product` returns `0` on any exception, which is also a valid low score, so a failed call and a genuinely bad match look identical downstream. I tried fixing this alongside the direct-visual-comparison change and reverted both together, so it's unresolved. I'd also revisit the cap of 10 scoring calls per search: it exists to stay under Gemini's 15 RPM free-tier limit, not because 10 is the right number of candidates.
